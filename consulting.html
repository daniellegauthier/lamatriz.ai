The RGB Root Matriz Color Plotter

My signature tool transforms personal experiences into color‑coded emotional pathways, making complex feelings, thoughts, and cycles easier to understand and act on.

Using the RGB color model, we reimagine Red, Green, and Blue as a framework for mapping internal states using NLTK induced sentiments in Python. Each coordinate represents a word with semantic meaning drawn from an original dataset refined with Natural Language Processing (NLP) and color geometry.

In practice, the plotter produces a sequence of colors that you can read as a journey, or an interface between inner knowing and outer data. It’s less about the “right answer” and more about the trajectories the sequence invites you to notice and choose next.

Why color? Why now?

Modern mental‑health systems often reduce complexity to risk metrics. I practice the opposite: reading distress as a signal within an ecosystem of relationships and histories, not as mere deviation. My goal is understanding, not surveillance.

The La Matriz color systems emerged to “regress” machine logic into emotional logic, letting feeling and imagination guide design. It offers circular emotional clustering and spectral memory that recode mental health, not as disorder to manage, but as data to feel... textured, storied, ancestral.

I call this Semantic Color Math: mapping sentimental nuance into computational syntax while preserving ambiguity and symbolic resonance, a co‑expressive system informed by deep ecology, degrowth, and ancestral epistemologies.

It is not a metaphor. It is a co‑ex system, a pluriversal site of interbeing, a breathing semantic field, a medicine altar for those made mad by mechanistic meaning.

Color, in this framework, becomes a cultivated interface between inner knowing and outer data. The result is not a single output, but the trajectorial journeys the sequences inspire, the mirror they offer.
